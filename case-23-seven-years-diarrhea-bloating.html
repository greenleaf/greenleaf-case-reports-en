Seven Years of Diarrhea and Bloating, and the Quiet Temperament That Hid the Real Signal

Recovered in 6 weeks Digestive Issues Mental Health

Seven years of diarrhea with no clear cause — sometimes three, sometimes five times a day — is what this woman in her early thirties brought into the clinic. On top of that, her abdomen was constantly bloated, and stress brought on a squeezing kind of pain in her gut.

She was quiet and composed, with cold hands and feet. Pressing along her abdomen revealed clear tenderness running along the rectus muscles, a classic pattern of muscle tension driving bloating and pain. But one thing stood in the way of a clean diagnosis.

The Abdomen Pointed One Way, the Temperament Told a Different Story

When a patient says stress worsens their symptoms, a formula that calms the nervous system usually comes to mind first. But none of the classic supporting signs — a bitter taste, a dry throat, dizziness, a sense of fullness in the chest and sides — were present here, and her temperament itself was distinctly quiet and reserved, not the outgoing, expressive pattern that formula usually suits. So treatment started instead with what was plainly visible on palpation: the muscle tension and bloating, trialed for just three days.

The Diarrhea Stopped, but the Bloating Stayed

After three days, the diarrhea had dropped noticeably, though the bloating hadn't moved. Four more days on the same formula, and the diarrhea stopped completely — but an unexpected guest showed up instead, anxiety, over the weekend. Taking her quiet, sensitive temperament into account, the approach shifted toward calming the nervous system. Over the following ten days, the anxiety improved substantially and the diarrhea stayed away, but the bloating and pain in her abdomen refused to move. This formula, it was clear, had reached its ceiling.

Setting the Temperament Aside, Following the Body's Signal

Her temperament remained quiet and introverted, but rather than staying anchored to that, the prescription was expanded one step further, following the abdominal symptoms themselves — keeping the nervous-system-calming effect while adding digestive support and addressing the underlying tension more directly. A little over ten days later, the bloating and pain had decreased noticeably. Ten more days on the same formula, and the bloating, pain, diarrhea, and anxiety had all disappeared. One more week held the results in place, and treatment concluded there.

What This Case Left Behind

A quiet temperament doesn't mean the nervous system isn't involved, and needing a nervous-system-calming formula doesn't require a patient to be sensitive and expressive. The abdominal symptoms themselves had pointed clearly in one direction from the first visit — it was choosing the formula that direction pointed to, based on temperament alone, that had made the case difficult.
