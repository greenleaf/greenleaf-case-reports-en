Erectile Dysfunction and Depression Together — Traced to Liver Heat and Resolved Over Three Months

Improvement over 12 weeks Men's Health Mental Health

For some people, the day after drinking doesn't just bring physical heaviness — it brings a mood that sinks along with it. And on those very days, everything else seems to feel worse too. That was exactly the pattern this patient described. He came in for erectile dysfunction, but depression was always close behind it, and both symptoms intensified whenever he drank.

What a Hand on the Skin Revealed

During the exam, placing a hand on the patient's skin revealed something notable — his skin ran noticeably warmer than what's typical for someone his age. He hadn't particularly noticed this himself, but it lined up closely with his long-standing struggle to tolerate heat.

The Liver as a Heat Factory

The liver is an organ that generates a substantial amount of heat while metabolizing various substances — and it produces even more when processing alcohol or sugar-heavy foods. In this patient's case, the liver appeared to be generating more heat than usual, and that excess heat was assessed as affecting the venous flow returning to the liver. This disrupted flow, in turn, appeared to affect circulation in the pelvic area — interfering with the blood flow regulation an erection depends on.

Depression as the Body's Alarm

The depressive mood turned out to be one of the more interesting pieces of this case. Rather than a purely psychological issue, it can be understood as the brain's response to an excess-heat state building up in the body — a kind of alarm signaling that something in the body's balance is off. That's why, on days when drinking pushed the internal heat even higher, both the erectile dysfunction and the depressed mood got noticeably worse together.

Starting by Cutting Off the Source

Treatment began by reducing the direct sources of that excess heat. Alongside alcohol, sugary foods like soda, juice, chocolate, and ice cream also feed heat production in the liver, so the patient was guided to cut back on those. From there, herbal medicine aimed at clearing the accumulated internal heat was introduced, along with acupuncture centered on the meridian associated with the liver.

Three Months, Two Symptoms Resolving Together

Over three months of treatment, both the erectile dysfunction and the depressive mood improved together. What looked like two unrelated symptoms turned out to trace back to the same underlying pattern of excess internal heat — which made their parallel improvement make complete sense.
