Digestive Issues

Five Years of Diarrhea and Restless Legs After Menopause

Recovered in 5 weeks Digestive Issues

Nancy had a calm voice. No rush in it at all — she laid out her symptoms one by one, in order. Menopause had hit five years earlier. Before that, she'd actually leaned toward constipation. But after menopause, her stool gradually loosened until it settled into ongoing diarrhea.

"And my legs shake every night. Going on seven years now."

Hunger or fatigue made her unusually irritable, she added. She carried extra weight in her lower body, and her skin was dry enough to flake white. She barely sweated at all. Falling asleep came easily, but she woke like clockwork at 3 or 4 a.m. and couldn't get back down.

An answer from outside the textbook

Treating diarrhea usually starts with one fork in the road: is this a cold-type pattern or a heat-type pattern? Nancy didn't fit cleanly into either. The bitter taste in her mouth hinted at a stress-driven pattern, but her temperament was too calm, too even-keeled, for that to be a good fit.

What actually caught my attention was something else entirely: the extra weight, the near-absence of sweat, skin dry enough to flake. That combination usually signals fluid and waste sitting stagnant just under the skin, between skin and muscle. The formula built for that pattern is normally reserved for people with strong constitutions — good appetite, good sleep, good digestion. Nancy's sleep was broken and her energy was middling, so I hesitated.

But looking again, her irritability and the 3 a.m. wake-ups could just as easily be read as heat building up from that same stagnation under the skin. And the diarrhea itself could be the same stagnant fluid, forced out through the wrong channel. It was an unconventional call, but I went with the formula meant to clear that surface-level stagnation.

News over the phone

Nancy lived too far away to come in for the follow-up, so we checked in by phone a week later.

"The diarrhea's stopped. My stool's normal now. And the leg shaking is noticeably better."

Her fatigue had eased, and sleep had improved — she still woke, but fell back asleep quickly. I sent another month's worth.

A month after that, she happened to be in the area and stopped by in person.

"My stool's completely normal now. My legs still shake a bit if I sit or walk for a long time, but it doesn't get in the way of anything."

The Herbs Behind This Formula

The formula prescribed here included:

  • Ephedra (Ma Huang) — clears fluid and waste that's built up just beneath the skin, pushing it out through sweat or urine. Often used for a puffy, dry-skinned constitution like Nancy's.
  • Cinnamon twig (Gui Zhi) — helps circulation extend all the way out to the body's surface.
  • Apricot kernel (Xing Ren) — works alongside the ephedra to clear stagnant dampness.
  • Gypsum (Shi Gao) — cools heat that's built up from the stagnation.
  • Licorice root, ginger, and jujube (red date) — keep the formula's various actions working together smoothly.
