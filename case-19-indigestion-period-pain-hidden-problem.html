Three Years of Indigestion and Period Pain, and the Real Problem Hiding Underneath

Recovered in 4 weeks Digestive Issues Uterine Conditions

A woman in her thirties came in with three years of poor digestion, recurring abdominal pain with diarrhea, and severe menstrual pain. Stress reliably made everything worse.

She ran cold, with cold hands and feet, and her lower abdomen turned especially cold during her period. Yet when she was tense, in heat, or had a drink, heat would rush up into her face and upper body instead. Her digestion was quite weak — eating even slightly more than usual, or facing something stressful, would leave her whole abdomen bloated and tight. Her bowel movements came roughly every other day, on the loose side, tending toward diarrhea. She sighed frequently. Her periods were irregular and tended to run late, arriving with clots and significant pain.

The first lead: stress and circulation intertwined

At her first visit, she framed her main concerns around indigestion and period pain. Both a stress-sensitive nervous system and a circulation issue behind her menstrual pain stood out clearly, so treatment began with a formula that addressed the nervous tension and the pelvic circulation together.

A week later, she reported her digestion felt somewhat better, and the same formula continued for another week.

Digestion is improving — so why does the abdominal pain linger?

After two rounds of that formula, digestion had clearly improved, but the abdominal pain and diarrhea were still there. At this point, it looked like her stress response was driving more of the picture than her cold sensitivity, so the circulation-focused piece was set aside in favor of a formula that addressed the nervous tension more directly.

A week after that, digestion was noticeably better still, but the diarrhea and abdominal pain kept coming and going.

The moment the real problem came into focus

Then something unexpected happened. While talking with another patient who had referred her, it came up that this patient's older sister had such persistent abdominal pain and diarrhea that people around her had given her a nickname for it. Checking in with the patient herself confirmed it: the recurring abdominal pain and diarrhea had actually been a bigger, longer-standing issue than the indigestion she'd led with.

When stress triggers the gut's muscles to clench and spasm reflexively, abdominal pain and diarrhea often show up together — and this looked like exactly that pattern. Treatment shifted once more, this time aimed directly at releasing that tension in the gut.

Confirmed by phone

A week later, a follow-up call confirmed that the abdominal pain and diarrhea had stopped completely. What began as treatment for indigestion and period pain ended up, through an offhand comment in conversation, resolving what turned out to be the real underlying issue all along.
