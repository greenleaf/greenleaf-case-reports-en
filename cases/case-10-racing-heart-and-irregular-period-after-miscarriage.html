Anxiety, a Racing Heart, and a Period That Wouldn't Settle After a Miscarriage

Recovered in 3 weeks Uterine Conditions Fertility

When she came into the clinic in her thirties, her body was sending two signals at once, and they didn't seem to belong to the same story.

She'd had a miscarriage a month earlier, followed by a D&C. Since then, her period had been coming twice a month, heavy, with clots. But separate from the physical toll of that, something else had shown up — anxiety with no clear trigger, a racing heart, moments that felt close to panic. There were times she felt so lightheaded and anemic she thought she might faint.

She had a small, slender build, and carried herself with a calm, bright energy — someone who'd been through something hard and was still holding herself together well. But after meals, gas built up fast, and she filled up on just a few bites.

The blood was replenished — but not everything eased

At first, the picture seemed clear. With all that bleeding, her body's reserves were running low, so the plan was to stop the bleeding and rebuild what had been lost — refill the tank, in a sense.

Acupuncture alongside a short course of an herbal formula did exactly that within three days. Her fatigue eased, the bleeding stopped. So far, so predictable.

But the racing heart and the anxiety hadn't moved an inch. The tank was refilled, but an alarm somewhere inside her kept going off anyway.

Which comes first — the anxiety, or the racing heart?

"Does the anxiety come first, or does your heart start racing first?"

It sounded like a small question, but the answer would point in an entirely different direction. She said her heart raced first.

With that answer in mind, I checked her abdomen again, and this time found what I was looking for — a band of muscle pulled taut across her middle. The bloating after meals made sense now too. A racing heart that came first, a tight band across the belly, discomfort after every meal — three signals, one direction.

This wasn't about an empty tank anymore. It was about a cord running through the center of her body that had been pulled too tight for too long — a tension that was making her heart race and her belly swell at the same time. The treatment shifted to loosen that instead.

Three days, then ten more

Three days later, the racing heart and the anxiety had noticeably eased. The bloating after meals was almost gone.

Ten more days on the same formula, and the symptoms disappeared one after another, in sequence. Her period returned to a regular rhythm, and the flow went back to normal.

After that, she kept coming in weekly for acupuncture — just maintenance. Then, a few months later, she let me know she was pregnant. She went on to deliver safely, and every so often, she still sends word.
