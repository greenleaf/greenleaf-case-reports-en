The Catching Feeling When Raising an Arm, Resolved by Reaching a Gap Hands Can't Access

Improvement over 3 weeks Pain

Every time this patient reached up to grab something off a shelf, something caught deep inside the shoulder, right at the same spot. Pushing harder never helped — the arm simply wouldn't go past that point. The same catching feeling showed up, in exactly the same place, while getting dressed or reaching overhead.

A Narrow Gap Between the Shoulder's Roof and Its Tendons

At the top of the shoulder, a bony projection from the shoulder blade forms a kind of roof, and the tendons that move the arm pass underneath it. The gap between this roof and the tendons is called the subacromial space. Under normal circumstances, there's enough room in this space for the tendons to glide smoothly every time the arm lifts. In this patient's case, the space had narrowed for some reason, so every attempt to raise the arm pinched the tendon against the roof above it, producing that same catching sensation and pain at the exact same point each time.

The Spot That Didn't Change, Even After the Muscles Released

Treatment began by assuming a rotator cuff muscle problem, and releasing that tension. Much of the patient's other discomfort did improve. But the arm still caught at the exact same spot. That lingering restriction, even after the muscle tension had eased, was the signal that the real problem wasn't the muscle — it was the gap the tendon passes through.

A Space Hands Simply Couldn't Reach

The subacromial space is an extremely narrow gap, bordered above by the shoulder's bony roof and below by tendon and joint tissue. No amount of pressing or massaging this patient's shoulder from the outside could reach it directly. But a thin, precise needle could reach directly into that narrow space. Where a hand had essentially no access at all, acupuncture became an exceptional pathway in.

A Week That Changed Everything

Once treatment targeted the subacromial space directly, this patient's range of motion opened up dramatically — as if the restriction had never been there. That change happened within just one week of starting treatment. From there, continued treatment of the surrounding muscles and ligaments further solidified the recovery, for a total treatment course of three weeks.

The Power of Finding the Exact Point of Catching

For this patient, identifying that the catching point wasn't in the muscle at all, but in a space hands could never reach, turned out to be the key to recovery.

The Herbs Behind This Formula

This case was treated with acupuncture alone; no herbal medicine was used.
