An IVF Pregnancy Alongside Uterine Fibroids — Overcoming a Placental Blood Flow Crisis and Preeclampsia to Meet Her Daughter at 36 Weeks

Placental blood flow restored in 2 weeks Fertility Uterine Conditions

This mother began her IVF journey carrying two sizeable uterine fibroids. Her overall health was good, but the presence of the fibroids alone was a persistent source of anxiety. Right after her first IVF attempt failed, she began combining acupuncture and herbal medicine to prepare her body again — work focused on improving circulation around the uterus and lifting her overall condition. On her second attempt, she became pregnant.

The Crisis Begins: Blood Flow to the Placenta Falters

After clearing the higher-risk early weeks and getting a clean NIPT result, it seemed like she could finally exhale — until sudden bleeding began. Tissue around uterine fibroids tends to have uneven blood vessel distribution and circulation compared to normal uterine tissue. When this imbalance persists, the problem doesn't always stay contained at the local tissue level around the fibroid — it can spread to affect circulation at the vascular level supplying the placenta.

That's exactly what happened. About a month into intermittent bleeding, an ultrasound found that blood flow to the placenta was barely detectable. When placental blood flow isn't sufficient, the oxygen and nutrients reaching the baby decrease, which shows up directly as growth delay. She was told that if the same picture persisted at a follow-up scan two weeks later, continuing the pregnancy might no longer be possible.

Two Weeks of Treatment, Every Single Day

Having been told there wasn't much more Western medicine could offer at that point, she began making the long trip to the clinic every day. Treatment during this period focused on releasing the localized circulation problems held around the fibroid tissue, and restoring blood flow at the vascular level that carries through to the placenta. Daily acupuncture, consistent herbal medicine, and two weeks of holding on — and then, on the follow-up ultrasound, placental blood flow had returned to a normal range.

An Unexpected Second Challenge

There was barely time to catch her breath before gestational diabetes and a sharp rise in blood pressure — preeclampsia — appeared next. This reflects the body's blood vessels struggling to keep up with the demands of pregnancy, with pressure regulation across the entire circulatory system becoming unstable. The baby continued growing at a normal pace after this, but remained about three weeks behind where she should have been. Her obstetrician had considered delivery around 30 weeks, but she and her treatment team pushed to keep the baby growing inside for as long as they safely could.

36 Weeks, and Finally Meeting Her Daughter

She reached 36 weeks, and a healthy baby girl weighing just over 2kg was born. Both mother and baby were healthy. From the uncertainty of starting IVF with fibroids, through a placental blood flow crisis, through preeclampsia — it was a long and demanding road. But staying attentive to every signal the body sent, and stepping in exactly when it was needed, made this outcome possible. More than anything, it was this mother's refusal to give up that carried the entire journey through.
