Waking Up Every Night for Two Years — Plus an Itchy Scalp No One Could Explain

Recovered in 4 weeks Mental Health

Robin made a great first impression — soft-featured, warm-eyed, the kind of person you'd peg as easygoing within thirty seconds of meeting her. But underneath that, she'd been fighting insomnia every night for two years.

"Falling asleep isn't the problem. It's staying asleep. I wake up and it's like I never slept at all."

On stressful workdays, even falling asleep was a struggle. Underneath it all sat a low hum of anxiety that never quite switched off. Lately her scalp had gotten unbearably itchy too, with dandruff flaking up out of nowhere. A few other things stood out once we dug in: a dry mouth, a tight, heavy feeling in the chest, frequent bathroom trips. She carried some extra weight, but it wasn't firm — more like puffiness, like her body was holding onto water it didn't need.

Two problems tangled together

Two separate issues were layered on top of each other here. One was stress sitting like a lump in her chest. The other was fluid that wasn't circulating properly and kept pooling in the wrong places. The puffiness, the frequent urination, the scalp trouble — all pointed to that second issue.

There were formulas for each problem individually, but using just one risked solving only half the picture. A formula aimed at the chest tightness wouldn't touch the fluid retention, and a formula built for fluid circulation would likely leave the chest tightness untouched. So I combined the two.

A week later — and one unexpected change

Robin came back a week later looking noticeably lighter.

"The chest tightness is gone. The scalp itching's gone. I'm barely waking up at night. I can't remember the last time getting up in the morning was this easy."

Then she added something that caught my attention.

"And this is weird, but I stopped craving sugar completely."

Once her body's circulation found its footing again, a habit she hadn't even mentioned as a symptom quietly disappeared along with everything else. I sent her home with three more weeks of herbs. The next time she came in, it was for a sore lower back — but while she was there, I checked in on the original complaints. The chest tightness, the insomnia, the itching, the anxiety: all still gone.
