A Boy Treated for Bedwetting a Decade Ago Returns as a College Student

Recovered in 4 weeks Mental Health Pediatrics

Ten years ago, a ten-year-old boy came in who struggled to control his temper and had significant attention difficulties. He'd already been diagnosed with ADHD and was on medication for it, and wet the bed frequently at night. He said he couldn't handle cold — yet he preferred cold water, couldn't stand tight clothing around his neck, and had trouble falling asleep.

The First Attempt Missed the Mark, but the Body's Clues Pointed the Way

The initial approach targeted his urinary and emotional symptoms with a fluid-circulation-supporting formula. After six weeks, his temper improved somewhat and his urinary symptoms got noticeably better, but not fully resolved. A second formula, tried for another six weeks, improved his sleep but did little for the urinary issue.

Recognizing Heat Hiding Behind Apparent Cold Intolerance

This is where an important shift happened. Revisiting the fact that although he claimed cold intolerance, he actually preferred cold water and disliked heavy clothing — this wasn't a sign of fluid stagnation at all, but a sign his body was running hot internally. Switching direction entirely toward cooling internal heat brought remarkable results: within two weeks, the bedwetting resolved completely, and after four more weeks, his attention difficulties had improved dramatically as well.

Ten Years Later, a Familiar Pattern Resurfaces

Last January, that boy — now a college student — returned. He'd been irritable all day, every day, unable to control his temper. On examination, he showed a clear tendency toward tearfulness, chest tightness, and significant tenderness when the area below his ribs was pressed. Tracing back when the symptoms began, it turned out they'd gradually emerged not long after he moved out on his own. A closer look at his diet revealed frequent takeout meals and a habit of drinking soda regularly — excess sugar and heat were once again at the root of the problem.

A Decade of Experience Made for a Faster Recovery

This time, the approach combined cooling internal heat with a formula that also addressed the chest tightness directly. Most symptoms resolved within just two weeks, and treatment was concluded after two more weeks as a preventive measure.

Same Constitution, Different Life Stage

One person's underlying constitution can send similar signals across very different life stages. At age ten, a heat-heavy constitution showed up as bedwetting and inattention. At twenty, that same constitution showed up as irritability and chest tightness. The surface symptoms shift with age and circumstance, but recognizing the underlying pattern makes it possible to find the right path to treatment far more quickly and precisely.
