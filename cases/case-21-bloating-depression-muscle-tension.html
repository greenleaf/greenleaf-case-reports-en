A Year of Bloating and Low Mood, and the Muscle Tension That Herbs Couldn't Fully Release

Recovered in 7 weeks Digestive Issues Mental Health

A man in his twenties came in with a year of uncomfortable abdominal bloating. He had a bowel movement daily, but the sense of relief never quite came. Perhaps because of this, he'd been tired and down for a while, and when the bloating got bad, it brought headaches and made urinating uncomfortable.

He wasn't especially sensitive to cold or heat, though if anything he leaned slightly toward heat. His whole abdomen felt bloated, sometimes tight enough in the chest to notice, and lying down to sleep often brought on palpitations. His mouth was frequently dry, and pressing on his abdomen revealed a clearly tense band of muscle on one side.

Starting from the tension in the abdominal muscle

Based on the muscle tension found on abdominal palpation and his clear low mood, treatment began with a formula aimed directly at releasing that tension.

A few days later, though, he came back with something unexpected: the formula had left his eyes heavy and his whole body exhausted, to the point he'd had to stop taking it. The bloating itself hadn't really changed. Suspecting one particular herb in the mix might have been too strong for him, that herb was removed and a modified version was prescribed instead.

What the unexpected reaction was actually pointing to

A few days later, the same heavy fatigue showed up again. That's when it became clear the fatigue wasn't coming from the herb originally suspected — it was coming from a different one, an herb that works on regulating nervous system tension.

At that point, a detail from the very first visit resurfaced: he'd mentioned that lying down to sleep often brought on palpitations. Asked again whether he also felt irritable or on edge, he confirmed that he did. Palpitations paired with irritability pointed toward a completely different kind of formula — one built around warming the body and gently easing muscle tension, rather than releasing it directly. His mild heat sensitivity had been enough to rule that direction out too quickly the first time around.

Treatment shifted entirely, toward warming the body and softening the tension rather than cutting through it.

Tension returning after a tattoo

Within days of the new formula, bloating dropped noticeably, everything felt like it was settling downward, and fatigue eased considerably. By the next visit, he could feel his stomach actively moving again, bloating had dropped sharply, and both the palpitations and irritability were easing.

Then, a few days after getting a tattoo, his irritability spiked for the whole day and the bloating ticked up slightly too. One physical stressor had briefly stirred up tension that had been settling down. The same formula continued while things were monitored.

The last bit of tension that herbs alone couldn't reach

At the next visit, he was slowly improving again but hadn't quite returned to where he'd been before the tattoo. The irritability was gone, but fatigue was significant, and the tension in the abdominal muscle could be felt once more. It seemed the herbs alone weren't quite enough to fully release this last remaining tension.

So the tense muscle itself was needled directly. The response at that spot was strong — a sign of just how much tension had been sitting there.

Everything resolved

At the visit following the needling, the bloating had disappeared completely, the fatigue was gone, and he reported feeling full of energy instead. Both the irritability and palpitations had resolved. A week later, at the final visit, every remaining symptom had cleared.
