Pain

Daily Headaches, a Stomach That Wouldn't Settle, and Cold Hands That Never Warmed Up

Recovered in 6 weeks Pain

Sujin walked in rubbing her arms. Thin frame, wide eyes, pale skin, and a nervous energy she couldn't quite hide.

"I never used to feel the cold like this. It's not even winter and my hands and feet won't warm up."

Something about the pace of her days — always on her feet, always managing people — had started wearing her down about a year earlier. The moment anything stressed her out, her stomach would seize up. Headaches showed up almost daily. And yet, oddly, heat kept surging up into her face and chest. Cold and heat, running through the same body at the same time. Sleep didn't come easily, and when it did, she'd wake up again and again through the night. Her periods were painful, and she sighed constantly, like something was sitting on her chest.

Three suspects, one culprit

A combination like this can point in a few different directions at once. It could be a headache rooted in genuine, bone-deep cold. It could be chronic inflammation in the gut itself. Or it could be long-term stress that's backed up somewhere in the body's circulation and never properly cleared. All three looked plausible on paper.

The tell, in the end, was her temperament. Sujin was anxious, yes, but not irritable or sharp-edged — she was careful and analytical instead. She listened to every explanation closely, took in details, asked precise follow-up questions. That kind of composure pointed away from a straightforward cold-type headache or a gut inflammation issue, and toward stress that had backed up and stalled her circulation. The one open question was whether a formula built for that kind of stagnation could handle cold this severe.

I settled on a formula meant to clear the stalled circulation while warming her up at the same time.

A month and a half later

Two weeks in, the change was obvious. The headaches had nearly disappeared. Her body felt warmer, and sleep came much more easily. Digestion improved too. Only the fatigue held on.

After another month on the herbs, she came back and told me:

"I'm not cold anymore. Digestion's good, I've put on three kilos. I still get a bit of indigestion when I'm stressed, but it's manageable now."

Then she added:

"I've started going back to the gym."

A body that had spent over a year rubbing its own arms for warmth was, finally, choosing to move on its own.
