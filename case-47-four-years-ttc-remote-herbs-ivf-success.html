Four Years, Two Failed IUIs, and the Herbal Care That Changed Her First IVF Cycle

Conceived after 16 weeks of care Fertility

She had been trying to conceive for four years, ever since surgery to remove an ovarian cyst. Living in Toronto, she had already gone through a course of acupuncture and herbal treatment there, but nothing seemed to change, and the process left her more exhausted than hopeful. On a visit to see family in Vancouver, she decided to try one more assessment — this time at Greenleaf.

She was honestly skeptical going in. Chinese herbal medicine doesn't come with a lab number you can watch move, so it's hard to know if it's doing anything at all. But partway through her first batch of personalized herbs, she noticed something concrete: the swelling in her legs — swelling she hadn't even realized was there until it was gone — had resolved.

Surgery Can Leave More Behind Than the Cyst Itself

Ovarian cyst surgery removes the cyst, but the disruption it leaves in the pelvic region's circulation and fluid balance doesn't always resolve on its own. When blood flow and lymphatic drainage through that area stay sluggish, it can show up as fluid retention elsewhere in the body — like the legs — and, more importantly, can keep the reproductive organs from getting the steady blood supply they need to support conception. The first formula was built to clear that stagnation and get circulation moving properly again.

Two Rounds of IUI, and a Formula That Kept Evolving

After finishing the first batch, she tried intrauterine insemination (IUI) twice. Neither attempt succeeded. Rather than treating this as a setback, the herbal formula was refined for the next two batches to shift focus toward building a healthier uterine lining and supporting egg quality — the two factors that matter most heading into a more involved fertility procedure.

The First IVF Cycle: 9 Eggs, 7 Fertilized, 3 Frozen

By the time she finished her third batch of herbs, she moved forward with in vitro fertilization (IVF). Nine eggs were retrieved, seven fertilized successfully, and three were frozen for future use. The very first transfer resulted in a pregnancy — and she went on to give birth in her early thirties. Her fertility specialist confirmed that both she and her husband were otherwise healthy, with nothing further that Western medicine alone could address.

A Year of Care, Coordinated From a Distance

Because she lived in Toronto, all of this was managed remotely — herbal formulas adjusted and shipped based on regular phone check-ins that continued for over a year, right up until she gave birth. There was no in-person acupuncture involved at any point, only the herbal treatment and consistent follow-up.

What This Case Left Behind

Fertility support doesn't always follow a straight line — a first formula may need to be adjusted more than once, and other treatments like IUI or IVF often run alongside it rather than after it. What mattered here was staying with the process long enough for the formula to be refined to what her body actually needed, and having that supported by consistent follow-up even from another city. In-person acupuncture alongside the herbal treatment may well have added even more — something she noted herself, wishing she'd had the chance to try both together.
